    2. The kernel and its simple dependencies.

  2. Caesar [SATELLITE] magnum iis numerum obsidum imperat

    3. Note that the satellite is a perfect participle. It agrees with gender, number, and case with Caesar. So:

    • Q: Why isn't it dependent on Caesar?
    • A. Particples agreeing with the subject of a verb are most often predicative; they tell us more about the circumstances of the event indicated by the verb than about any characteristics of the subject.
    • Consider these two English sentences:
      • The boy swimming near the reef was eaten by a shark.
      • Swimming near the reef, the boy was eaten by a shark
    • In the first sentence, the participle is attributive and gives more information about "the boy."
    • In the second sentence, the participle is predicative or circumstantial and gives information about the whole event.

      • In the same way, arbitratus does not specify some quality of Caesar, but explains more about the whole event indicated by imperat.

  3. Caesar arbitratus imperat

    4. Arbitratus itself has an OBJ and we are not surprised to see that it is a clause. Why?

  5. Hoc sibi satis oportune accidisse arbitratus [SATELLITE]
