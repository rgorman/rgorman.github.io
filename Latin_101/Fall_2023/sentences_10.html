Translate these sentences into English and be ready to explain your work.

1
Carthāginem īre iussī sunt.
  • Carthāginem: pLN of a city in Africa, Carthāgō, Carthāginis, f. = "Carthage"; ACC X without a preposition can = "to X" when X is the name of a city
  • īre: eō, īre, īvī, ____, = "go"
  • iussī sunt: iubeō, iubēre, iussī, iussus = "order/command"; verb frame: SBJ orders ACC X to infinitive Y. In the passive, X becomes SBJ (nom) and Y remains an infinitive.
2
mē Pompeius Capuam īre voluit.
  • mē: SBJ of an infinitive is usually ACC
  • Pompeius: PrN, Pompeius, Pompeī, m.
  • Capuam: pLN of a town in Italy, Capua, Capuae, f.
  • īre: eō, īre, īvī, ____, = "go"
  • voluit: volō, velle, voluī, ____, = "wish/want"; verb frame: SBJ wants X, where X is an infinitive clause
3

  • tē: SBJ of an infinitive is usually ACC

  • nōlō: nōlō, nolle, nōluī; verb frame: SBJ does not want X, where X in an infinitive clause
