Latin Accentuation Cheatsheet

1. Accent Placement Depends on Syllable Count

  • Monosyllabic words: No choice for accent (e.g., rex, hic).
  • Disyllabic words: Accent falls on the first syllable (e.g., līber, mālum).

2. For Words with 3 or more Syllables

Accent falls on the penult (second-to-last syllable) if:

  • The penult is long by nature (contains a long vowel or diphthong).
  • The penult is long by position (vowel followed by two consonants).

Otherwise, the accent falls on the antepenult (third-to-last syllable).

Examples:
amīcus → amīcus
audīre → audīre
dominus → dominus
populus → populus

3. Common Long Vowels & Diphthongs

  • Long vowels: ā, ē, ī, ō, ū
  • Diphthongs: ae, au, oe, ei, eu
