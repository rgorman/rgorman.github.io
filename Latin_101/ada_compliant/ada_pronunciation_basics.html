Classical Latin Pronunciation

This guide provides a basic overview of the classical pronunciation of Latin, which is based on the educated speech of Rome in the 1st century B.C.E.

Vowels

Latin has five vowels (A, E, I, O, U) that can be either short or long. The length of the vowel can change the meaning of a word. Long vowels are typically marked with a macron (a bar over the vowel), and short vowels may be marked with a breve (a U-shaped symbol over the vowel).

Vowel Pronunciation (like in English) Example
Ā, ā (long) like 'a' in 'father' fāma (fame)
A, a (short) like 'u' in 'but' or 'a' in 'apart' amo (I love)
Ē, ē (long) like 'e' in 'they' or 'a' in 'able' tēla (web)
E, e (short) like 'e' in 'pet' et (and)
Ī, ī (long) like 'i' in 'machine' or 'ee' in 'see' vīnum (wine)
I, i (short) like 'i' in 'sit' in (in)
Ō, ō (long) like 'o' in 'go' or 'oa' in 'boat' ōrātor (orator)
O, o (short) like 'o' in 'pot' porta (gate)
Ū, ū (long) like 'u' in 'flute' or 'oo' in 'moon' lūna (moon)
U, u (short) like 'u' in 'put' sub (under)

Consonants

Most Latin consonants are pronounced similarly to their English counterparts, but some have very specific pronunciations that differ from common English usage. The most significant differences are listed below.

Consonant Pronunciation (like in English) Example
C always a hard 'k' sound, never an 's' sound. Cicerō (pronounced "Kikero")
G always a hard 'g' sound, like 'g' in 'go'. gēns (pronounced "gens")
I (when used as a consonant) like 'y' in 'yet'. This occurs at the beginning of a word before a vowel (e.g., Iūlius) or in the middle of a word between vowels. iūs (pronounced "yus"), maior (pronounced "ma-yor")
S always a voiceless 's' sound, like 's' in 'sing', never a 'z' sound. rosa (pronounced "rosa")
T always a hard 't' sound, never a 'sh' sound. nātiō (pronounced "na-ti-o")
V like 'w' in 'win'. via (pronounced "wia")
GN like the 'ny' in 'canyon' or the 'ñ' in Spanish. magnus (pronounced "ma-nyus")
QU like 'qu' in 'queen'. The 'u' is a consonant 'w' sound. aqua (pronounced "a-kwa")
GU like 'gw' in 'linguist'. The 'u' is a consonant 'w' sound. lingua (pronounced "lin-gwa")
X like 'ks' in 'box'. rēx (pronounced "raiks")
