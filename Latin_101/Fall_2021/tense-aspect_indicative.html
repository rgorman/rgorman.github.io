Tense and Aspect of Latin Indicatives

Study guide for Latin Tense and Aspect. (Future time, Prospective aspect, and Debitive aspect are currently obscured).

A table showing the relationship between time and aspect in Latin Indicatives. Future time and Debitive/Prospective aspects are currently hidden.
Aspect / Time Past time Present time Future time
Completed aspect
Active Latin: pluperfect tense
Eng.: -ed(have) + -en(verb-x)
example: "had bitten"
Passive Latin: perfect participle + Imperfect indicative of sum
Eng.: -ed(have) + -en(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "had been bitten"
Active Latin: perfect tense
Eng.: -s(have) + -en(verb-x)
example: "has bitten"
Passive Latin: perfect participle + Present indicative of sum
Eng.: -s(have) + -en(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "has been bitten"
Active Latin: future perfect tense
Eng.: will + -base(have) + -en(verb-x)
example: "will have bitten"
Passive Latin: perfect participle + Future indicative of sum
Eng.: will + base(have) + -en(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "will have been bitten"
Simple aspect
Active Latin: perfect tense
Eng.: -ed(verb-x)
example: "bit"
Passive Latin: perfect participle + Present indicative of sum
Eng.: -ed(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "was bitten"
Active Latin: present tense
Eng.: -s(verb-x)
example: "bites"
Passive Latin: present tense
Eng.: -s(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "is bitten"
Active Latin: future tense
Eng.: will + -base(verb-x)
example: "will bite"
Passive Latin: future tense
Eng.: will + base(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "will be bitten"
Progressive aspect
Active Latin: imperfect tense
Eng.: -ed(be) + -ing(verb-x)
example: "was biting"
Passive Latin: imperfect tense
Eng.: -ed(be) + -ing(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "was being bitten"
Active Latin: present tense
Eng.: -s (be) + -ing(verb-x)
example: "is biting"
Passive Latin: present tense
Eng.: -s(be) + -ing(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "is being bitten"
Active Latin: future tense
Eng.: will + base(be) + -ing(verb-x)
example: "will be biting"
Passive Latin: future tense
Eng.: will + base(be) + -ing(be) + -en(verb-x)
example: "will be being bitten"
Prospective aspect
Active Latin: future active participle + Imperfect indicative of sum
Eng.: -ed(be) + going to/about to + base(verb-x)
example: "was about to/going to bite"
No Passive
Active Latin: future active participle + present indicative of sum
Eng.: -s(be) + going to/about to + base(verb-x)
example: "is about to/going to bite"
No Passive
Active Latin: future active participle + future indicative of sum
Eng.: will + base(be) + going to/about to + -base(verb-x)
example: "will be about to/going to bite"
No Passive
Debitive aspect
No Active
Passive Latin: future passive participle + imperfect indicative of sum
Eng.: -ed(have) + to be + -en(verb-x)
example: "had to be bitten"
No Active
Passive Latin: future passive participle + present indicative of sum
Eng.: -s(have) + to be + -en(verb-x)
example: "has to be bitten"
No Active
Passive Latin: future passive participle + future indicative of sum
Eng.: will + base(have) + to be + -en(verb-x)
example: "will have to be bitten"
* Note: Advanced tenses (Future) and aspects (Prospective, Debitive) are currently hidden from this view.
