Finite Complement Clauses Focus: Aspect is neutralized; Tense indicates time.
Subjunctive Form Tense Sememe Aspect Sememe Semantic Function (Example)
Present Non-Past None Contemporaneous to Primary main verb.
Optō ut vincat.
I pray that he win.
Imperfect Past None Contemporaneous to Historical main verb.
Optāvī ut vinceret.
I prayed that he win.
Perfect Non-Past None Prior to Primary main verb.
Optō ut vīcerit.
I pray that he has won.
Pluperfect Past None Prior to Historical main verb.
Optāvī ut vīcisset.
I prayed that he had won.
Cum-Temporalia (Circumstantial) Clauses Focus: Subjunctive mirrors Indicative Tense + Aspect.
Subjunctive Form Tense Sememe Aspect Sememe Semantic Function (Example)
Imperfect Past Progressive Past action in progress (Contemporaneous).
Cum vinceret, laetus eram.
When he was winning, I was happy.
Pluperfect Past Completed Past action finished (Anterior).
Cum vīcisset, laetus eram.
When he had won, I was happy.
