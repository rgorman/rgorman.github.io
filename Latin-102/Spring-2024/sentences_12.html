Latin 102
Infinitives and gerunds

1
Ego ā cōnsiliō fugiendī absum.
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"cōnsiliō
  • cōnsiliō: = "plan"
  • fugiendī: NEU SING GEN, gerund → "escaping"
  • absum: construction → SBJ is distant from/has nothing to to with ab +ABL(X)
2
Nūllum locum praetermittō agendī.
  • locum: here = "opportunity"
  • praetermittō: = "pass over/skip over
  • agendī: NEU SING GEN, gerund → "acting/doing someting"
3
Etiam illa causa est nōn veniendī.
  • veniendī: NEU SING GEN, gerund → "coming"
4
Nihil necesse est mihi dē mē ipsō dīcere.
  • necesse est: this expression often has an infin. SBJ
  • mihi: DAT(X) = "to/for X"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • dīcere: SBJ of (necesse) est
5
Difficile est mē id sentīre, quod tū velīs.
  • Difficile est: this expression often has an infin. SBJ
  • sentīre: SBJ of (difficile) est
  • mē: ACC → SBJ of sentīre
  • quod: NEU SING ACC
  • velīs: this subjunctive indicates a potential situation, = "may wish/may want"
6
Difficile est omnia persequī et nōn necessārium.
  • Difficile est: this expression often has an infin. SBJ
  • persequī: present infinitive deponent, SBJ of (difficile) est.
7
Quod ad Xenōnem scrīpsistī, valdē mihi grātum est.
  • Quod: here a subordinating conjunction, introducing a noun clause (usually, a clause acting as a SBJ or OBJ); = "the fact that ..."
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X)
  • Xenōnem: MASC SING ACC, PrN of a Greek, Xenō, Xenōnis, m.
  • mihi: DAT(X) = "to/for X"
8
Loquor enim dē doctō homine, cui vīvere est cōgitāre.
  • Loquor: present indicative deponent
  • doctō: adjective = "educated"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • cui: MASC SING DAT, relative pronoun; DAT(X) = "to/for X"
  • vīvere: SBJ of est
  • cōgitāre: predicate nominal of est
9
Negōtium magnum est nāvigāre atque id in mēnse Quīntilī.
  • Negōtium: = "business/job/problem"
  • nāvigāre: SBJ of est
10
Ita rēs sē habet, ut ex nātūrā vīvere summum bonum sit.
  • Ita rēs sē habet, "the matter has itself thus" is a common idiom for something like "the situation is this..."
  • ut: ut + SUBJ = finite complement clause; translate ut ... sit "that ... is."
  • ex: prep. + ABL(X) may = "according to X"
  • vīvere: predicate nominal of sit
  • summum: NEU SING NOM, adjective = "highest"
11
Labor multō maior est tōtam causam, quam partem, dīcere.
  • multō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by X extent/by X"
  • causam: = "cause" ≈ "political cause" or "legal case"
  • quam: subordinating conjunction introducing a comparison clause, = "than"
  • dīcere: predicate nominal of est
12
Ā mē nūllum tempus praetermittitur dē tuīs rēbus et agendī et cōgitandī.
  • Ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "through the agency of/by X"
  • praetermittitur: = "lose/waste/skip over"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "concerning/about X"
  • et ... et: "both ... and"
  • agendī: NEU SING GEN, gerend → "acting/taking action"
  • cōgitandī: NEU SING GEN, gerend → "thinking/planning"
13
Oppianicī pecūnia post iūdicium factum ab iūdice ablāta est.
  • Oppianicī: MAS SING GEN, PrN, Oppianicus, Oppianicī, m.
  • post iūdicium factum: = "after the decision having been made" = "after the decision had been made"
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "through the agency of/by X"
  • ablāta est: perfect passive of auferō → simple past passive
14
Ad eās ego eī litterās nihil rescrīpsī, nē grātulandī quidem causā.
  • Ad: prep. + ACC(X) can = "in response to/to X"
  • eās: FEM PLUR ACC, see, is, ea, id
  • nē grātulandī quidem: nē X quidem = "not even X"
  • grātulandī: gerund, NEU SING GEN
  • causā: ABL(causa) often = "for the sake of"
15
Neque nunc mittendī tamen ūlla causa fuit praeter eam, dē quā tibi rescrībī voluistī.
  • mittendī: gerund, NEU SING GEN
  • praeter: prep. + ACC(X) = "beyond/except for X"
  • eam: FEM SING ACC, see is, ea, id
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "concerning/about X"
  • quā: relative adjective pattrns
  • rescrībī: no SBJ is expressed for this infinitive; in English add "something" or the like as SBJ
  • voluistī: construction → SBJ wants/desires X, where X is a complementary infiitive clause
16
Sequātur necesse est, ut, cuius in potestāte summum bonum, in eiusdem vīta beāta sit.
  • Sequātur: the phrase necesse est can have a subjunctive without a subordinating conjunction as its SBJ. Sequātur necesse est ut = "it is necessary that it follows that" = "it necessarily follows that"; "follow" is used here in the logical sense. ≈ "be a logical consequence"
  • cuius: relative adjective
  • cuius in potestāte: = "in whose power"
  • eiusdem: see īdem, eadem, idem
  • in eiusdem: an ellipsis for in eiusdem potestāte: = "in that person's power"
17
Ego mē in hāc omnī causā facile intellegēbam pugnandī cupidīs hominibus nōn satis facere.
  • in: prep. + ABL(X) = "in X"
  • causā: = "(legal) case" or "(political cause")
  • facile: adverb
  • intellegēbam: construction → SBJ understands that X, where X is an indirect statement clause
  • pugnandī: gerund, NEU SING GEN
  • cupidīs: adjective = "eager"; construction → "eager for X" where X is GEN
  • hominibus: DAT(X) = "for/to X"
  • satis facere: "do enough"
  • facere: the SBJ of this infinitive is not explicitly given; it is assumed to be the same as the SBJ of the matrix verb
18
Post hanc habitam contiōnem duābus tribusve hōrīs optātissimī nūntiī et litterae vēnērunt.
Post hanc habitam contiōnem is a dominant participle construction. Translate "after this gathering was held."
19
Aeduī, cum sē suaque ab iīs dēfendere nōn possent, lēgātōs ad Caesarem mittunt rogātum auxilium.
Aeduī refers to a group of people. Caesarem is a PN. rogātum is a participle (also called a supine) expressing purpose: "to ask for."
20
Nec vērō ipse posteā tempus ūllum intermīsī dē rē pūblicā nōn cōgitandī sōlum sed etiam agendī.
nōn x sōlum sed etiam y is a focus construction: not only x but even y.
