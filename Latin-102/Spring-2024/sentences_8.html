Latin 102
Indirect Statement Clauses

1
id tū eum nōlle mihi scrīpsistī.
  • nōlle: present infinitive active → simple [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb
  • eum: see is, ea, id; SBJ of the infinitive
  • scrīpsistī: verb frame → SBJ writes OBJ(X), where X is indirect statement clause
2
sciō enim tē familiārem esse Othōnum.
  • sciō: verb frame → SBJ knows OBJ(X), where X is indirect statement clause
  • tē: SBJ of the infinitive
  • familiārem: = "friend"
  • esse: present infinitive active → progressive [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb; verb frame: SBJ is PNOM(X), where X is same case (and gender and number if possible) of SBJ
  • Othōnum: MAS PLUR GEN, PrN of members of a Roman family, Othōnēs, Othōnum, m. plural
3
Gaium Coelium quaestōrem hūc venīre audīstī.
  • Gaium Coelium: MAS SING ACC, PrN of a Roman, Gaius Coelius, Gaī Coelī, m.; SBJ of infin.
  • venīre: present infinitive active → progressive [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb
  • audīstī: verb frame → SBJ hears OBJ(X), where X is an indirect statement clause
4
potuisse certē tenērī illam prōvinciam sciō.
  • potuisse: perfect infinitive active → completed [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb; completed present = simple past for purposes of translation
  • tenērī: present infinitive passive → [Ø-aspect] [Ø-time] passive
  • prōvinciam: SBJ of potuisse.
  • sciō: verb frame → SBJ knows OBJ(X), where X is indirect statement clause
5
sciō enim mē nihil tibi grātius facere posse.
  • sciō: verb frame → SBJ knows OBJ(X), where X is indirect statement clause
  • mē: SBJ of posse.
  • nihil: NEU SING ACC = "nothing"
  • grātius: NEU SING ACC adjective
  • facere: present active infinitive → [Ø-aspect] [Ø-time] active
  • posse: present active infinitive → simple [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by a matrix verb
6
ad Pompēium contendit, ut adesse Caesarem nūntiāret.
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Pompēium: MAS SING ACC
  • contendit: = "hurry/move in a hurry"
  • ut ... nūntiāret: ut + Subjunct. → a "that/so that" clause;
  • nūntiāret: verb frame → SBJ announces OBJ(X), where X is an indirect statement clause
  • adesse: = "be near"; present infinitive active → simple [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by a matrix verb
  • Caesarem: SBJ of adesse
7
senātōrēs multōs esse Rōmae nōs quoque audierāmus.
  • senātōrēs: SBJ of esse.
  • esse: present infinitive active → progressive [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb
  • Rōmae: FEM SING LOC
  • quoque: focuses emphasis on the preceding word; nōs quoque = "we too"
  • audierāmus: verb frame → SBJ hears OBJ(X), where X is an indirect statement clause
8
eō ipsō diē Syrācūsae ā Mārcellō captae esse dīcuntur.
  • eō: see is, ea, id
  • diē: ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • Syrācūsae: FEM PLUR NOM, PrN name of a city in Sicily, Syrācūsae, Syrācūsārum, f. plural
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "be the agency of/by X"
  • Mārcellō: MAS SING ABL, PrN of a Roman, Mārcellus, Mārcellī, m.
  • captae esse: perfect infinitice passive → completed [time(X)] passive, where X is indicated by a matrix verb; when the matrix verb is passive (as dīcuntur), it may be better to translate the verb of an indirect statement with an English infinitive, = "to have been captured"
9
Pompēium scīs temporibus illīs nōn saepe in senātū fuisse.
  • Pompēium: MAS SING ACC, SBJ of fuisse.
  • scīs: verb frame → SBJ knows OBJ(X), where X is an indirect statement clause
  • temporibus: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • in: prep. + ABL(X) = "at/in/on X"
  • fuisse: perfect infinitive active → completed [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by a matrix verb
10
tunc ducēs prīncipēsque Nerviōrum colloquī sēsē velle dīcunt.
  • ducēs: = MAS PLUR NOM, = "leaders"
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
  • Nerviōrum: MAS PLUR GEN, PrN of a Gallic people, Nerviī, Nerviōrum, m. plural
  • colloquī: present infinitive deponent → [Ø-aspect] [Ø-time] active
  • sēsē: SBJ of velle; reflexive referring to SBJ of a matrix verb
  • velle: present infinitive active → simple [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb; verb frame → SBJ wants OBJ(X), where X is complementary infinitive clause
  • dīcunt: verb frame → SBJ says OBJ(X), where X is indirect statement clause
11
Gaius Caesar inimīcissimus esse meae salūtī dīcēbātur.
  • Gaius Caesar: MAS SING NOM, PrN name of a Roman
  • inimīcissimus: superlative adjective = "most hostile"
  • esse: present infinitive active → progressive [time(X)] active, where X is determined by matrix verb; when the matrix verb is passive (as is dīcēbātur), it may be better to translate the verb of an indirect statement with an English infinitive: "to be"
12
litterīs scrīptīs, audīvī Dolābellam in Ciliciam vēnisse cum suīs cōpiīs.
  • scrīptīs: PERF PASS PARTICIPLE, FEM PLUR ABL
  • audīvī: verb frame → SBJ hears OBJ(X) where X is indirect statement clause
  • Dolābellam: SBJ of vēnisse.; MAS SING ACC, PrN of a Roman, Dolābella, Dolābellae, m.
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "into X"
  • Ciliciam: FEM SING ACC, PrN of a region in Asia Minor, Cilicia, Ciliciae, f.
  • vēnisse: perfect infinitive active → completed [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by matrix verb
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
13
audierat Pompēium per Maurētāniam cum legiōnibus iter in Hispāniam facere.
  • audierat: verb frame → SBJ hears OBJ(X) where X is indirect statement clause
  • Pompēium: MAS SING ACC
  • per: prep. + ACC(X) = "through X"
  • Maurētāniam: FEM SING ACC, PrN of a region in Africa; Maurētānia, Maurētāniae, f.
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "into X"
  • facere: present infinitive active → progressive [time(X)] active, where X is an indirect statement clause; iter facit = "SBJ makes a journey"
14
illud quidem perlibenter audīvī ex eōdem Chrȳsippō, tē esse Caesarī familiārem.
  • illud: NEU SING ACC; cataphoric pointing at the indirect statement clause; translate "that, namely X" where X is the later indirect statement clause
  • quidem: adverb that emphasizes the previous word
  • perlibenter: adverb = "with great pleasure"
  • audīvī: verb frame → SBJ hears OBJ(X) where X is indirect statement clause
  • eōdem: MAS SING ABL
  • Chrȳsippō: MAS SING ABL, PrN of a Greek, Chrȳsippus, Chrȳsippī, m.
  • tē: SBJ of esse.
  • esse: present infinitive active → progressive [time(X)] active, where X is indicated by a matrix verb; verb frame: SBJ is PNOM(X), where X is same case (and gender and number if possible) of SBJ
  • familiārem: = "friend"
15
intellēxī ex tuīs litterīs tē ex Turrāniō audīsse ā mē prōvinciam frātrī trāditam.
  • tē: SBJ of infin.
  • Turrāniō: PrN of a Roman, Turrānius, Turrānī, m.
  • audīsse: perfect infin. = audīvisse
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) can = "through the agency of/by X"
  • prōvinciam: SBJ of infin.
  • trāditam: = trāditam esse, = perfect infinitive passive
16
Hirtius ad mē scrīpsit Sextum Pompeium Cordubā exīsse et fūgisse in Hispāniam citeriōrem.
  • Hirtius: PrN of a Roman, Hirtius, Hirtī, m.
  • Sextum Pompēium: SBJ of infin.; PrN of a Roman, Sextus Pompēius, Sextī Pompeī, m.
  • Cordubā: PrN of a town in Spain, Corduba, Cordubae, f.; ABL(X) without prep. can = "from X"
  • exīsse: perfect infin.
  • fūgisse: perfect infin.
  • citeriōrem: comparative adj.
17
periūcundae mihi fuērunt litterae tuae, quibus intellēxī tē perspicere meam in tē pietātem.
  • quibus: see relative adjective patterns; ABL(X) without prep. can = "by means of X"
  • tē: SBJ of infin.
  • pietātem: = "loyalty"
