Latin 301
Futures and other Unreals

1
Sed erunt omnia facilia, sī valēbis.
  • erunt: future tense; construction → SBJ(X) is PNOM(Y), where X and Y are same number, gender, and case
  • valēbis: = "be well/be healthy; future tense → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
2
Dē rē Piliae quod scrībis, erit mihi cūrae.
  • Dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "concerning/about X"
  • Piliae: FEM SING GEN; PrN of a Roman, Pilia, Piliae, f.
  • quod scrībis: autonymous relative clause (has no expressed antecedent); functions as SBJ of erit
  • quod: see relative adjective; = "what/the thing that"
  • erit: future tense → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
  • mihi: DAT(X) = "for/to X"
  • cūrae: DAT(X) = "a cause for X"
3
Vellem dictum esset ab eōdem etiam dē Diōne.
  • Vellem: imperfect subjunctive → simple present unreal; construction → SBJ would have + Verb-EN; an unreal can be used to express a desire more politely
  • dictum esset = ut dictum esset; finite complement clause = "that it would have been said/spoken." In this passage, dīcō is used very generally to mean "to discuss a topic." Thus, dictum esset ... dē Diōnē = "that a discussion would have been held about Dion."
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "through the agency of/by X"
  • etiam: = "also"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "concerning/about X"
  • Diōne: MAS SING ABL, PrN of a Greek, Diō, Diōnis, m.
4
Neque tū hoc dīcere audēbis nec, sī cupiās, licēbit.
  • Neque ... nec: = "neither ... nor"
  • hoc: NEU SING ACC
  • audēbis: future tense; construction → SBJ dares to X, where X is complementary infinitive clause
  • cupiās: present subjunctive → simple present poetential; construction → SBJ may/might desire
  • licēbit: = "be permitted/allowed"; future tense → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
5
Sed id facilius cōnsequar, sī ad mē in Ciliciam vēneris.
  • id: NEU SING ACC
  • facilius: comparitive adverb
  • cōnsequar: future deponent; → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "in X"
  • Ciliciam: PrN of a region in Asia Minor, Cilicia, Ciliciae, f.
  • vēneris: future perfect; in a clause subordinate to a future, English often uses a present verb to refer to a future time; = "you come"
6
Quā rē usque ad Nōnās Maiās tē in Formiānō exspectābō.
  • Quā rē: = "therefore"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to/until X"
  • in: prep. + ABL(X) = "at/in/on X"
  • Formiānō: NEU SING ABL, PrN of an estate in Italy, Formiānum, Formiānī, n.
  • exspectābō: = "wait for"; future → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
7
Tē convēnissem, sī eō, quō cōnstituerās, venīre voluissēs.
  • convēnissem: = "to meet"; pluperfect subjunctive = "past unreal"; → "would have + -EN(X)"
  • eō: adverb = "to that place/there"
  • quō: relative adverb = "to which place/where"
  • cōnstituerās: = "agree"
  • voluissēs: = "be willing"; pluperfect subjunctive = "past unreal" → "had + -EN(X)
8
Plūra scrīberem, sī iam putārem libenter tē legere posse.
  • Plūra: NEU PLUR ACC, comparative = "more things/more"
  • scrīberem: imperfect subjunctive → progressive present unreal → "would be -ING(X)"
  • putārem: imperfect subjunctive → progressive present unreal: in a clause subordinate to a present unreal, English often prefers a simple past to an unreal expression; = "thought"; cnstruction → SBJ thinks OBJ(X), where X is an indirect statement clause
  • tē: SBJ of posse
  • legere: = "read"
  • posse: construction → SBJ is able to/can OBJ(X), where X is complementary infinitive clause
9
Litterās ad eum scrīpsī, quās cum accēperis, faciēs, quod volēs.
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • eum: see is, ea, id
  • quās: see relative adjective patterns
  • cum: subordinating conjunction = "when"; note that Latin allows combining a relative pronoun and a subordinating conjunction in the same clause.
  • accēperis: future perfect; translate with completed present
  • faciēs: = "do"; future → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
  • quod: see relative adjective patterns; this relative has no antecedent; it is autonymous; = "what/the thing that"
  • volēs: = "wish"; future tense → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
10
Omnibus appāruit, nisi ille fuisset, Spartam futūram nōn fuisse.
  • appāruit: construction → SBJ(X) appears to DAT(Y), where X is an indirect statement clause
  • nisi: = "unless/if not"
  • fuisset: past unreal → "SBJ had not existed"
  • Spartam: FEN SING ACC, SBJ of futūram fuisse
  • futūram fuisse: simple unreal → "would have existed"
11
Hīs dē rēbus plūra ad tē in eā epistulā scrībam, quam ipsī Quīntō dabō.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "concerning/about X"
  • plūra: comparitive, = "more things"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • in: prep + ABL(X) = "at/in/on X"
  • scrībam: future tense → simple future → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
  • quam: FEM SING ACC, relative adjective
  • ipsī: MAS SING DAT
  • Quīntō: MAS SING DATM PrN of a Roman, Quīntus, Quīntī, m.
  • dabō: future tense → simple future → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
12
Plūribus verbīs tēcum agerem, nisi prō mē apud tē rēs ipsa loquerētur.
  • Plūribus: comparative, = "more"
  • verbīs: ABL(X) without a prep. = "by means of/with X"
  • tēcum: = cum tē
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
  • agerem: simple present unreal → "woud + BASE(X), where X is content verb
  • tēcum agerem: agō cum + ABL(X) = negotiate with/have a discussion with X"
  • prō: prep. + ABL(X) = "in favor of X"
  • apud: prep. + ACC(X) can = "with X"
  • loquerētur: progressive present unreal → were -ING(X), where X is content verb
13
Aliī, quasi corpus nūllum sit hominis, ita praeter animum nihil cūrant.
  • nūllum: predicative adjective = "non-existent/null"
  • sit: present subjunctive → present potential = "was"
  • praeter: prep. + ACC(X) = "beyond/except for X"
  • cūrant: construction → SBJ cares about X, where X is ACC
14
Intereā, quae ad dignitātem tuam pertinēre arbitrābor, dīligenter cūrābō.
  • quae: relative adjective ; SBJ of pertinēre; it has no antecedent, = "the things which"
  • quae ... arbitrābor: the entire relative clause is the OBJ of cūrābō
  • ad: prep. + ACC(C) = "to X
  • arbitrābor: future deponent → simple future active; in subordinate clause translate as simple present; construction → SBJ thinks that X, where X is an indirect statement clause
  • cūrābō: future → simple future → will + BASE(X) where X is content verb; construction → SBJ will take care of X, where X is a autonymous relative clause
15
Haec sī et agēs et sentiēs, eris nōn modo cōnsul, sed magnus cōnsul.
  • Haec: NEU PLUR ACC
  • agēs: future tense → simple future, = "will do"
  • sentiēs: future tense → simple future = "will think about"
  • eris: future tense → simple future; construction → SBJ is PNOM(X), where X is same number, gender and case as implied SBJ
  • nōn modo: = "not only"
16
Nunc, ut ad tē anteā scrīpsī, sī ad nōs vēneris, cōnsilium tōtīus reī capiēmus.
  • ut: = "as/just as"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • nōs: MAS PLUR ACC
  • vēneris: future perfect; translate as simple or completed present = "come/have come"
  • cōnsilium ... capiēmus: = "to make a plan"
  • capiēmus" future tense → simple future → will + BASE(X), where X is content verb
17
Cum illūc vēnerō intellēxerōque, quod negōtī sit, tum, ad quōs diēs reditūrus sim, scrībam ad tē.
  • Cum: subord. conj. = "when"
  • illūc: adv. = "to that place"
  • vēnerō: future perfect: translate as simple or completed present
  • intellēxerō: future perfect: translate as simple or completed present
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
  • quod negōtī sit: headless (autonymous) relative clause; functions as OBJ of intellēxerō
  • quod: NEU SING NOM: = "what/the thing which"
  • ad ... sim: OBJ of scrībam
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "on X"
  • reditūrus sim: prospective present → -S-form(BE) + -ING-form(X), where x is the content verb
18
Cum haec legēs, habēbimus cōnsulēs et omnia perspicere poteris dē Caesare, dē Pompēiō, dē ipsīs iūdiciīs.
  • Cum: = "when"
  • haec: NEU PLUR ACC
  • legēs: future tense; in a subordinate clause may be translated with simple present
  • habēbimus: future tense → simple future → will + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
  • poteris: future tense, = "will be able"
  • iūdiciīs: = "law courts"
19
Sī quid habērem, quod ad tē scrīberem, facerem id plūribus verbīs et saepius, sed nunc quae sint negōtia, vidēs.
  • quid: NEU SING ACC: = "anything"
  • habērem: imperfect subjunctive → simple present unreal → -ED-form(X), where X is the content verb
  • scrīberem: imperfect subjunctive → simple present unreal; in this context = "might write"
  • facerem: imperfect subjunctive → simple present unreal → would + BASE(X), where X is the content verb
  • id: NEU SING ACC
  • verbīs: ABL(X) without prep. = "with/by means of X"
  • negōtia" = "problems"
  • vidēs: construction → SBJ sees X, where X is an indirect question clause
20
Nōs in Formiānō esse volumus usque ad prīdiē Nōnās Maiās, sed eō sī ante eam diem nōn vēneris, Rōmae tē fortasse vidēbō.
Formiānum is a place name.
