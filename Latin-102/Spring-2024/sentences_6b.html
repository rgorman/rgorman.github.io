Latin 102
Circumstantial and Attributive Participles

1
hoc spērāns legiōnēs trīs ex castrīs ēdūcit.
  • spērāns: present participle → progressive active
  • trīs: = "three," FEM PLUR ACC
  • ex: prep. + ABL(X) = "out of/from X"
2
Caelius profectus ad Caesarem pervēnit Thūriōs.
  • Caelius: MAS SING NOM, PrN of a Roman, Caelius, Caelī, m.
  • profectus: = "set out/depart"; perfect deponent → completed active → [having + V-EN]
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Thūriōs: MAS PLUR ACC, PrN of a town in Italy, Thūriī, Thūriōrum, m. plural
3
heri dederam ad tē litterās exiēns ē Puteolānō.
  • heri: adverb = "yesterday"
  • dederam: ≈ "send"
  • exiēns: present participle → progressive active
  • ē: prep. + ABL(X) = "out of/from X"
  • Puteolānō: NEU SING ABL, PrN of a country estate in Italy, Puteolānum, Puteolānī, n.
4
prīdiē Īdūs Fundīs accēpī tuās litterās cēnāns.
  • Īdūs: ACC(X) without prep. = "before X"
  • Fundīs: MAS PLUR ABL, PrN of a town in Italy, Fundī, Fundōrum, m. plural; ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • cēnāns: present participle → progressive active
5
hās tibi litterās exiēns in Tusculānum scrīpsī.
  • exiēns: present participle → progressive active
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Tusculānum: NEU SING ACC, PrN of a country estate in Italy, Tusculānum, Tusculānī, n.
6
inde per Ligurēs Appennīnum trānsiit petēns Etrūriam.
  • inde: adverb, = "from that place/from there"
  • per: prep. + ACC(X) = "through X"
  • Ligurēs: MAS PLUR ACC, PrN of an Italian people, Ligurēs, Ligurum, m. plural
  • Appennīnum: MAS PLUR ACC, PrN of a mountain range in Italy, Appennīnus, Appennīnī, m. = "the Appenines"
  • petēns: = "travel towards"; present participle → progressive active
  • Etrūriam: FEM SING ACC, PrN of a region in Italy, Etrūria, Etrūriae, f.
7
Pompeius idōneum locum nactus ibi cōpiās collocāvit.
  • Pompeius: MAS SING NOM. PrN of a Roman leader
  • nactus: = "find by chance/reach by accident"; perfect deponents → completed present = [having + V-EN]
  • collocāvit: = "put in position/station"
8
itinera fēcit multō maiōra fugiēns quam ego sequēns.
  • itinera: can = the distance travelled in one day = "a day's march"
  • multō: ABL(X) without prep. = "by X"; specifies a measure as in, for example, "taller by a foot"
  • fugiēns: present participle → progressive active
  • quam: subordinator introducing a comparison clause, = "than"
  • sequēns: present participle → progressive active
9
tamen cōnscendēns iam nāvem Ephesō Lāodicēam revertit.
  • cōnscendēns: = "board/go aboard"; present participle → progressive active
  • Ephesō: FEM SING ABL, PrN of a Greek city in Asia Minor, Ephesus, Ephesī, f. ABL(X) without prep. an = "from X"
  • Lāodicēam: FEM SING ACC, PrN of a town in Asia Minor, Lāodicēa, Lāodicēae, f.; ACC(X) without prep. = "to X"
10
reliquōs omnīs equitātū cōnsecūtī nostrī interfēcērunt.
  • equitātū: ABL(X) without prep. = "with X"
  • cōnsecūtī: = "catch up with/overtake" perfect deponent → completed active = "having + -EN(verb)"
  • nostrī: MAS PLUR NOM → substantive, = "our men"
11
quīntō Kalendās mane accēpī ā Dēmeā litterās prīdiē datās.
  • quīntō: ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • Kalendās: ACC(X) without prep. = "to/before X"
  • mane: adverb = "in the morning"
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • Dēmeā: MAS SING ABL, PrN of an enslaved person, Dēmea, Dēmeae, m.
  • datās: = "send"; perfect passive participle → completed passive
12
ēgressus inde, diēs noctēsque iter faciēns Taurum trānsiit.
  • ēgressus: perfect deponent → completed active = "having + -EN(verb)"
  • diēs: ACC(X) without prep. = "for/for the duration of X"
  • -que: coordinator, Xque = "and X"
  • noctēs: ACC(X) without prep. = "for/for the duration of X"
  • iter: = "journey/march"
  • faciēns: present participle → progressive active
  • Taurum: MAS SING ACC, PrN of a mountain range in Asia Minor, Taurus, Taurī, m.
13
prīnceps decima legiō per tribūnōs mīlitum eī grātiās ēgit.
  • prīnceps: secondary predication, = "(as a) leader" ≈ "first"
  • decima: = "tenth," decimus, decima, decimum
  • per: prep. + ACC(X) = "through X"
  • eī: MAS SING DAT, see is, ea, id
  • grātiās ēgit: grātiās agō = "I give thanks/I thank"; the person being thanked is in the DAT
14
eōdem diē lēgātī ab hostibus missī ad Caesarem dē pāce vēnērunt.
  • eōdem: = "the same,"
  • diē: ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "by/through the agency of X"
  • missī: perfect passive participle → completed passive
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
15
cōnfēcit prior iter Caesar atque contrā hostem aciem īnstruit.
  • prior: ="in front/first"; secondary predication.
  • iter: = "journey/march"
  • contrā: prep. + ACC(X) = "against X"
  • aciem: = "battle line"
16
tibi autem crēbrō ad mē scrībentī dē eius officiō nihil rescrīpsī.
  • crēbrō: adverb, = "frequently"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • scrībentī: present participle → progressive active
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • eius: see is, ea, id
  • nihil: NEU SING ACC
17
multās ā tē accēpī epistulās eōdem diē omnīs dīligenter scrīptās.
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • eōdem: = "the same,"
  • diē: ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • omnīs: a secondary predication. Here it further specifies which of the epistulās were dīligenter scrīptās.
  • scrīptās: perfect participle → completed passive
18
ipsī idōneum locum nactī reliquam noctis partem ibi cōnfēcērunt.
  • ipsī: MAS PLUR NOM
  • nactī: = "find by chance"; perfect deponent → completed active = "having + -EN(verb)"
  • cōnfēcērunt: verb frame: SBJ spends/finishes ACC(X,) where X is a length of time
19
ego dē prōvinciā dēcēdēns quaestōrem Coelium praeposuī prōvinciae.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • dēcēdēns: present participle → progressive active
  • Coelium: PrN of a Roman, Coelius, Coelī, m.
  • praeposuī: verb frame: SBJ puts OBJ1(X) in charge of OBJ2(Y), where X is ACC and Y is DAT
20
quō nūntiō Artaxerxēs commōtus Autophradatem in Cappadociam mittit.
  • quō: = "this" see relative adjective patterns; Latin relative words can sometime lose their subordinating force.
  • nūntiō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by/by means of X"
  • Artaxerxēs: MAS SING NOM, PrN name of the king of Persia, Artaxerxēs, Artaxerxis, m.
  • commōtus: perfect participle → completed past
  • Autophradatem: MAS SING ACC, PrN name of a Persian, Autophradatēs, Autophradatis, m.
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "into X"
  • Cappadociam: PrN of a region in Asia Minor
21
nōn enim sumus omnīnō sine cūrā venientis annī, etsī sumus sine timōre.
  • sine: prep. + ABL(X) = "without X"
22
proficīscēns enim ē Tusculānō octāvō Kalendās mīsī ad eum tabellāriōs.
  • ē: prep. + ABL(X) = "out of/from X"
  • Tusculānō: PrN of a country estate, Tusculānum, Tusculānī, n.
  • octāvō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • Kalendās: ACC(X) without a prep. can = "to/before X"
23
itaque cōnsilium mūtāvit et ad tē statim meā manū scrīptās litterās mīsī.
  • manū: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by means of/with X"
24
ipse paulum ex eō locō cum legiōne prōgressus ēventum pugnae exspectābat.
  • paulum: adverb = "a little ways"
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
  • prōgressus: perfect deponent in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
25
dē senātūs cōnsultō certior factus dēlēctum tōtā prōvinciā habēre īnstituit.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • certior factus: = "having become more certain"
  • dēlēctum: = "draft/levy"
  • prōvinciā: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
26
quibus rēbus commōtī lēgātī mīlitēs ex opere dēdūcunt, oppugnātiōne dēsistunt.
  • quibus: see relative adjective patterns. Relative adjectives can sometimes lose their subordinating function, = "this/that"
  • rēbus: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by means of/by X"
  • dēsistunt: verb frame = SBJ desists from ABL(X)
27
Cenabensēs paulō ante mediam noctem ex oppidō ēgressī flūmen trānsīre coepērunt.
  • Cenabensēs: PrN of a Gallic people, Cenabensēs, Cenabensum, m. plural
  • paulō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by/by the extent of X"
  • ante: prep. + ACC(X) = "before X"
  • ēgressī: perfect deponent in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
28
interfectus est etiam fortissimē pugnāns Crāstinus, cuius mentiōnem suprā fēcimus.
  • interfectus est: a single verb phrase, = perfect indicative passive etc.
  • fortissimē: superlative adverb
  • Crāstinus: PrN of a Roman, Crāstinus, Crāstinī, m.
  • suprā: adverb
29
illī nōn diū commorātī nec longius ab īnfimō colle prōgressī cōpiās in castra redūcunt.
  • commorātī: perfect deponent in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
  • longius: comparative adverb
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • prōgressī: perfect deponent in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
30
classem fugientem persecūtī sumus usque Sīdam, quae extrēma regiō est prōvinciae meae.
  • persecūtī sumus: a single verb phrase, = perfect indicative passive etc.
  • usque: adverb = "all the way (to)"
  • Sīdam: PrN of a town, Sīda, Sīdae, f.; ACC(X) without a prep. can = "to X"
  • quae: see relative adjective patterns

hīs nūntiīs litterīsque commōtus Caesar duās legiōnēs in citeriōre Galliā novās cōnscrīpsit.
