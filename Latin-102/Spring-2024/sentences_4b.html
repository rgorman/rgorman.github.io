Latin 102
Relatives, Conditionals, etc.

1
eae rēs grātiōrēs quam illae.
  • grātiōrēs: comparative, FEM PLUR NOM
  • quam: perhaps a coordinator, = "than"
2
mundō autem certē nihil est melius.
  • mundō: ABL(X) without a prep. can = "than X"
  • melius: comparative, NEU SING NOM
3
nihil est illī rē pūblicā cārius.
  • illī: DAT(X) can indicated a person affected by the situation, = "to/for X"
  • rē: ABL(X) without a prep. can = "than X"
  • cārius: comparative, NEU SING NOM
4
sī tū exercitusque valētis, bene est.
  • sī: subordinating conjunction
  • valētis: = "be well"
  • -que: coordinating conjunction, Xque = "and X"
5
nam nihil istō potest esse iūcundius.
  • istō: ABL(X) without a prep. can = "than X"
  • potest: verb frame → SBJ(X) is able to/can OBJ(Y), where X is NOM and Y is a complementary infinitive clause
  • iūcundius: comparative, NEU SING NOM
6
semper aequē mihi amīcus fuit ac sibi.
  • aequē: = "equally/in the same manner"
  • ac: coordinator; it introduces an equivalent comparison, = "as"
  • mihi ... sibi: DAT(X) = "to/for X"; the idea seems to be that of giving another person's interests the same value as are given to one's own
7
sē, simul ac potuit, ad Antipatrum recēpit.
  • simul ac: a complex conjunction (i.e., it consists of more than a single word), = "as soon as"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Antipatrum: MAS SING ACC, PrN of a Greek man, Antipater, Antipatrī, m.
  • recēpit: recipit sē: ≈ withdraw
8
nihil aliud fēcerant, nisi rem dētulērunt.
  • nisi: subordinating conjunction, = "if ... not/unless/except"
  • dētulērunt: = "report"; in English the SBJ (and tense) is often suppressed in a clause beginning with the conjunction "except": "except report"
9
sī enim es Rōmae, iam mē adsequī nōn potes.

  • sī subordinating conjuction, = "if"

  • Rōmae: FEM SING LOC
  • adsequī: verb frame → SBJ catches up with OBJ(Y), where Y is ACC
  • potes: verb frame → SBJ is able to/can OBJ(Y), where X is NOM and Y is a complementary infinitive clause
10
vōs nihil aliud nisi dē praesidiō rettulistis.

  • nisi subordinating coordinator, = "except."

  • rettulistis: = "report"; ellipsis (leaving out words) is very common when nisi is used.
11
ego minus saepe dō ad vōs litterās, quam possum.
  • minus: adverb, specifies saepe, = "less"
  • dō: ="give" ≈ "send"
  • quam: conjunction, introduces a comparison (non-equivalent), = "than"
12
nam Balbum posteā, quam tū es profectus, nōn vīdī.

  • quam introduces a comparison clause (non-equivalent). posteā ... quan = "after(wards) ... than" ≈ "after"

  • Balbum MAS SING ACC, PrN of a certain Roman man.

  • es profectus: a single compound predicate, the perfect indicative of proficīscor, = "set out"
13
hōc mihi grātius in istā prōvinciā facere nihil potes.

  • hōc: Abl(X) = "than X"

  • grātius: NEU SING ACC, a comparative adjectice

  • potes: verb frame → SBJ is able to/can OBJ(Y), where X is NOM and Y is a complementary infinitive clause
14
sed ego Thūcȳdidī crēdō, quod eius cīvitātis fuit.

  • quod is a causal conjunction, = "because"

  • crēdō verb frame → SBJ gives trust to OBJ(Y) where Y is DAT.

  • Thūcȳdidēs MAS SING DAT, PrN of a certain Greek man.

  • cīvitātis: a genitive can be used as a PNOM
15
Capuae Nōnīs Februāriīs esse volēbam, quia cōnsulēs iusserant.

  • Capua: FEM SING LOC, proper name of a certain city.

  • Nōnīs: ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • volēbam: verb frame → SBJ wants OBJ(X), where X is complementary infinitive clause
16
nōn tam multa in istīs rēbus intellegō, quam multa vīdī.

  • quam introduces a comparison clause (equivalence). Translate tam multa ... quam multa "as many ... as"

17
veniō ad recentiōrēs litterās, quamquam dē Serviō iam rescrīpsī.
  • quamquam → concessive clause.
  • Servius: MAS SING ABL; proper name of a certain Roman man.
18
tū, sī istīus modī sermōnēs ad tē dēlātī dē mē sunt, nōn dēbuistī crēdere.
  • dēlātī ... sunt: a single predivate, = "report"
  • dēbuistī: verb frame → SBJ ought to OBJ(X), where X is a complementary infinitive clause
19
nam, quās ex itinere ante, quam ex Asiā ēgressus es, ad mē litterās mīsistī, legī.
quās relative clause; quās can be an adjective as well as a pronoun. Here it is the attribute of litterās: quās litterās = "which letters." quam comparison clause (non-equivalence).
20
apertius autem haec agō ac saepius, quod Quīntus, meus frāter, lēgātus est Caesaris.
agō often means "do." quod causal clause.
21
minus multa ad tē scrīpsī, quod exspectābam tuās litterās ad eās, quās prīdiē dederam.
quod causal clause. quās relative clause.
22
haec quamquam nihilō meliōra sunt, tamen inānīs esse meās litterās, quam nūllās, māluī.
quamquam concessive clause. quam comparison clause (non-equivalence because of māluī)
23
etsī dederam Quīntō Mūciō litterās ad tē, tamen sine meīs litterīs eum ad tē venīre nōluī.
etsī concessive clause. Quīntus Mūcius is the proper name of a certain Roman man.
24
agitur enim nihil aliud in hāc causā, nisi ut nūllum sit in rē pūblicā pūblicum cōnsilium.
nisi conditional clause (translate as "except" here). Unlike English "none," nūllum can function as PNOM; translate here as "non-existent."
25
Mārcus Flaccus, quia cum Gāiō Gracchō contrā salūtem reī pūblicae fēcerat, ex senātūs sententiā est interfectus.
quia causal clause. Mārcus Flaccus and Gāius Gracchus are proper names of certain Roman men. ex ... sententiā means "according to the decision."
26
sed posteā, quam nōn nūllī prīncipēs ex eā cīvitāte ad Caesarem vēnērunt et dē suīs prīvātīs rēbus ab eō petere coepērunt, Indutiomarus lēgātōs ad Caesarem mittit.
quam comparative clause (non-equivalence). The double negative nōn nūllī means "some."
27
tuum cōnsilium vehementer laudō, quod nōn prius exercitum Apollōniā Dyrrachiōque mōvistī, quam dē Antōnī fugā audīstī.
cōnsilium probably best taken here as "judgment." quod causal clause. Apollōnia and Dyrrachium are proper names of certain towns; they are ABL of separation here. quamcomparative clause (non-equivalence).
28
quod ratiōne ūtitur, id melius est quam id, quod ratiōne nōn ūtitur; nihil autem mundō melius et ratiōne igitur mundus ūtitur.
ūtitur is one of a small number of verb that takes a ABL object. quam comparative clause (non-equivalence). mundō is ABL of comparison.
29
multās ūnō tempore accēpī epistulās tuās, quae mihi, quamquam recentiōra audiēbam ex iīs, quī ad mē veniēbant, tamen erant iūcundae.
quamquam concessive clause.
30
omnēs hostēs terga vertērunt neque prius fugere dēstitērunt, quam ad flūmen Rhēnum mīlia passuum ex eō locō circiter quīnque pervēnērunt.
quam comparative clause (non-equivalence). mīlia is ACC of extent.
31
cōnstituerāmus, ut ad tē anteā scrīpseram, obviam Cicerōnem Caesarī mittere, sed mūtāvimus cōnsilium, quia dē illīus adventū nihil audiēbāmus.
ut is a relative adverb ("as"). obviam is a adverb meaning "in the way of." It is used with a DAT indicating a person who will be met en route. quia causal clause.
32
Pompēius neque ā marī Dyrrachiōque discēdere volēbat, quod omnem apparātum bellī ibi collocāverat, neque mūnītiōnēs Caesaris prohibēre poterat.
quod causal clause.
33
nōs apud Alyziam, ex quō locō tibi litterās ante dederāmus, ūnum diem commorātī sumus, quod Quīntus nōs cōnsecūtus nōn erat.
ex quō locō: relative pronouns can also be adjectives ("from which place").
quod causal clause. cōnsecūtus ... erat counts as a single verb (erat is an auxilary).
34
Crassum Samarobrīvae praeficit legiōnemque attribuit, quod ibi impedīmenta exercitūs, obsidēs cīvitātum, litterās pūblicās, frūmentumque omne relinquēbat.
The verb frame of praeficit is SBJ puts ACC in charge of DAT. quod causal clause.
