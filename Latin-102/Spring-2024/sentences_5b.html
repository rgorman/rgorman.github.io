Latin 102
Temporal Relatives, Temporal clauses, etc.

1
cum Philogenēs Lāodicēam ad mē vēnisset, hās litterās dedī.
  • cum: subordinating conjunction, = "since/when"
  • Philogenēs: MAS SING NOM, PrN of a Greek man, Philogenēs, Philogenis, m.
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Lāodicēam: ACC(X) without prep. = "to X"; FEM SING ACC, PrN of a city in Asia Minor, Lāodicēa, Lāodicēae, f.
  • vēnisset: use aspect and time as for indicative
2

3

10
ubi hostēs ad lēgātōs exercitumque pervēnērunt, ūniversī sē ad pedēs prōiciunt.
  • ubi: relative adverb = "when"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
  • ūniversī: rather emphatic = "one and all"
  • sē: reflexive, refers to same entity as subject of prōiciunt
  • pedēs: = "[their] feet"
11

  • ubi: relative adverb = "when"
  • signa: = "standards/emblems"
  • coepērunt: verb frame → SBJ begins to X, where X is complementary infinitive
  • sēsē: reflexive, refers to the same entity as the subject of cōnferunt.
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "into X"
  • sēsē ... cōnferunt: = "carry oneself" ≈ "go"
