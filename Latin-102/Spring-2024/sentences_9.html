Latin 102
Indirect Question Clauses

1
videō, quid ēgerim.
  • quid: NEU SING ACC, = "what?"
  • ēgerim: perfect subjunctive → simple past/completed present
2
videō, quid agat.
  • quid: NEU SING ACC, = "what?"
  • agat: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
3
scrīpsit, quid fierī vellet.
  • quid: NEU SING ACC, = "what?"; SBJ of fierī.
  • fierī: infin. = "to be done"
  • vellet: imperfect subjuncive → progressive past
4
nunc, quae sint negōtia, vidēs.
  • quae: NEU PLUR NOM = "what?"
  • sint: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
5
sciō, quam sīs amīcīs iūcundus.
  • quam: interrogative adverb of degree, = "how" as in "how fast," "how interesting" etc.
  • sīs: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
  • amīcīs: DAT(X) = "to/for X"
6
vident, quantum in tē sit animī.
  • quantum: NEU SING NOM, interrogative adjective = "how much?"
  • sit: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
  • quantum ... animī: = "how much of courage" ≈ "how much courage"
7
quaesīvit, unde esset epistula.
  • unde: interrogative adverb, = "from where/whence"
  • esset: imperfect subjunctive → progressive past
8
quō quidem in cāsū sīmus, vidēs.
  • quō: MASC SING ABL, interrogative adjective = "what/which?"
  • in: prep. + ABL(X) = "in X"
  • cāsū: = "predicament/difficulty"
  • sīmus: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
9
prīmum quaerō, quī id scīre potuerit.
  • prīmum: adverb = "first/in the first place"
  • quī: interrogative adverb = "how/in what manner?"
  • id: NEU SING ACC, see is, ea, id
  • potuerit: perfect subjunctive → simple past/completed present; verb frame → SBJ is able/can X, where X is a complementary infinitive clause
10
factum sit necne, vehementer quaeritur.
  • necne: = nec + -ne; -ne is an interrogative particle and indicates a clausal question, "whether X or not X"; necne often comes at the end of an indirect question clause, just like English "or not?"
  • factum sit: = "happen"; perfect subjunctive of fiō, fit, → simple past/completed present
11
vidētis, quōs et quam multōs habeat Antōnius.
  • quōs: MAS PLUR ACC, interrogative pronoun = "who/whom?" or interrogative adjective = "which?"
  • quam: interrogative adverb = interrogative adverb of degree, = "how" as in "how fast," "how interesting" etc.
  • habeat: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
12
quanta multitūdō hominum convēnerit ad hoc iūdicium, vidēs.
  • quanta: FEM SING NOM, interrogative adjective = "how great?"
  • convēnerit: = "assemble/gather together"; prefect subjunctive → simple past/completed present
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "at X"
  • iūdicium: = "trial/court/courtroom"
13
nē dē Persārum quidem rēge magnō potes dīcere, beātusne sit.
  • nē ... quidem: focus adverbs putting emphasis on the phrase occuring between them, nē X quidem = "not even X"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • potes: verb frame→ SBJ is able/can X, where X is a complementary infinitive clause
  • beātusne: -ne is an interrogative particle and indicates a clausal question, "whether X", where X is the clause to which -ne is attached
  • sit: present subjunctive → simple present/progressive present
14
dē hōc prīmum quaerō, vēnerit ea rēs in hoc iūdicium necne.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • prīmum: adverb = "first/in the first place"
  • vēnerit: perfect subjunctive → simple past/completed present
  • ea: see is, ea, id
  • rēs: = "a case" [at law]
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "into X"
  • iūdicium: "trial/court/courtroom"
  • necne: = nec + -ne; -ne is an interrogative particle and indicates a clausal question, "whether X or not X?"; necne often comes at the end of an indirect question clause, just like English "or not?"
15
equitēs ā Fabiō missī, quantō rēs in perīculō fuerit, expōnunt.
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "through the agency of/by X"
  • Fabiō: MAS SING ABL, PrN of a Roman, Fabius, Fabī, m.
  • missī: perfect passive participle → completed Ø-time passive
  • quantō: interrogative adjective = "how much?"
  • fuerit: perfect subjunctive → simple past/completed present
16
etiam quaerēbant, cūr ego ex ipsō cursū tam subitō revertissem.
  • cūr: interrogative adverb = "why?"
  • ex: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • cursū: = "voyage/journey"
  • tam: qualifier, a type of adverb usually specifying adjectives and adverbs, = "so" as in "so fast," "so evil," etc.
  • subitō: adverb = "suddenly"
  • revertissem: pluperfect subjunctive → completed past
17
nunc quaerō, utrum vestrās iniūriās an reī pūblicae persequāminī.
  • utrum ... an are interrogative particles and indicate a disjunctive clausal question. They can be translated "whether ... or" or "... or."
  • persequāminī: = "avenge"; present subjunctive deponent → simple present active/progressive present active
  • vestrās ... an reī pūblicae: a coordinating conjunction such as an can only coordinate words with the same syntactic function; they do not need to belong to the same word class. Here an adjective and a genitive noun phrase both give more information about iniūriās. In other words, they both function as atributes of iniūriās and can therefore be coordinated.
