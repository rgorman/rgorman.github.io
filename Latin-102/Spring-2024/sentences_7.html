Latin 102
Participial Clauses 2

1
hōc quoque interfectō, exercitus summam imperī ad eum dētulit.
  • hōc: MAS SING ABL
  • quoque: adverb = "also"
  • interfectō: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • summam: as a substantive = "supreme command"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
2
ego tamen, Philotimī litterīs lēctīs, mūtāvī cōnsilium dē mulieribus.
  • Philotimī: MAS SING GEN, PrN of a Roman, Philotimus, Philotimī, m.
  • lēctīs: = perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • cōnsilium: = "plan"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
3
iam prope hieme cōnfectā, lēgātī ad eum prīncipēs Aeduōrum veniunt.
  • prope: adverb = "nearly"
  • cōnfectā: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • lēgātī: best understood as a secondary predication = "as ambassadors"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • eum: see is, ea, id
  • Aeduōrum: MAS PLUR GEN, PrN of a Gallic people, Aeduī, Aeduōrum, m. plural.
4
eō celeriter cōnfectō negōtiō, rūrsus in hīberna legiōnēs redūxit.
  • cōnfectō: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • rūrsus: adverb = "back"
  • hīberna: = "winter quarters"
5
hāc pugnā nūntiātā, lēgātōs ad Caesarem mīsērunt sēque eī dēdidērunt.
  • nūntiātā: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • sē ... dēdidērunt: = "surrendered themselves"
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
6
actīs hīs rēbus, litterae ā tē mihi redditae sunt, quibus ā mē cōnsilium petis.
  • actīs: perfect participle → completed passive = "(having been) done"
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • redditae sunt: = "was delilvered"
  • quibus: see relative adjective patterns; ABL(X) without prep. "at/in/on X"
  • cōnsilium: = "advice"
7
nūllam partem noctis itinere intermissō, in fīnis Lingonum diē quārtō pervēnērunt.
  • partem: ACC(X) without prep. = "for/for the duration of X"
  • intermissō: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "in/into X"
  • fīnis: = "boundaries" ≈ "territory"
  • Lingonum: MAS PLUR GEN, PrN of a Gallic people, Lingonēs, Lingonum, m.
  • diē: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
8
quibus rēbus cognitīs, mediā nocte silentiō profectus ad hostium castra mane pervēnit.
  • quibus: a relative adjective may sometimes function as a simple anaphoric modifier, without subordinating force; = "these/those"
  • cognitīs: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • nocte: ABL(X) without prep. may = "at/in/on X"
  • profectus: = "depart/set out"; perfect deponent → completed acative = "having + V-en"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "at/to X"
  • silentiō: adverb, = "quietly"
  • mane: adverb, = "in the morning"
9
itaque duābus cohortibus Ilerdae praesidiō relictīs, omnibus cōpiīs Sicorim trānseunt.
  • Ilerdae: FEM SING LOC, PrN of a town in Spain, Ilerda, Ilerdae, f.
  • praesidiō: DAT(X) = "for/for the purpose of an X"
  • relictīs: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • cōpiīs: ABL(X) without prep. = "with/accompanied by X"
  • Sicorim: MAS SING ACC, PrN of a River in Spain, Sicoris, Sicoris, m.
10
hāc ōrātiōne ab Dīviciācō habitā, omnēs, quī aderant, auxilium ā Caesare petere coepērunt.
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "by/by the agency of X"
  • habitā: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • Dīviciācō: MAS SING ABL, PrN of a Gallic man, Dīviciācus, Dīviciācī, m.
  • quī: see relative adjective patterns
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • petere: verb frame → SBJ seeks/asks for OBJ(X), where X is ACC
  • coepērunt: verb frame → SBJ begins to/starts to X," where X is a complementary infinitive clause
11
hōc proeliō trāns Rhēnum nūntiātō, Svebī, quī ad rīpās Rhēnī vēnerant, domum revertī coepit.
  • trāns: prep. + ACC(X) = "across X"
  • nūntiātō: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • Svebī: MAS PLUR NOM, PrN of a Germanic people, Svebī, Svebōrum, m. plural
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Rhēnī: PrN of a river, Rhēnus, Rhēnī, m.
  • domum: ACC(X) without a prep. = "to X"
  • coepit: verb frame → SBJ begins to/starts to X," where X is a complementary infinitive clause
12
acceptīs mandātīs, Rōscius cum Caesare Capuam pervēnit ibique cōnsulēs Pompeiumque invēnit.
  • acceptīs: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • Rōscius: MAS SING NOM, PrN of a Roman, Rōscius, Rōscī, m.
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with/in the company of X"
  • Capuam: FEM SING ACC, Capua, Capuae, f.
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
13
Germānī mūnītiōnēs nostrās ēgressī, complūribus interfectīs, sēsē ad suōs incolumēs recēpērunt.
  • Germānī: MAS PLUR NOM, PrN of a people, Germānī, Germānōrum, m. plural
  • ēgressī: perfect deponent participle = "having + EN(verb)"; = "depart from X," where X is ACC
  • complūribus: = "many"
  • interfectīs: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • suōs: substantive adj. = "their own men"
  • incolumēs: a secondary predication, = "safe" ≈ "safely"
  • sēsē ... recēpērunt: = "withdrew themselves" ≈ "withdrew" ≈ "went back to"
14
Cūriō cum omnibus cōpiīs quārtā vigiliā exierat, cohortibus quīnque castrīs praesidiō relictīs.
  • Cūriō: MAS SING NOM, PrN of a Roman, Cūriō, Cūriōnis, m.
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with/in the company of X"
  • vigiliā: ABL(X) without prep. = "at/in/on X"
  • castrīs: DAT(X) = "for/for the benefit of X"
  • praesidiō: DAT(X) = "for/for the purpose of an X"
  • relictīs: perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
15
hīs rēbus celeriter administrātis, ipse, cum prīmum per annī tempus potuit, ad exercitum contendit.
  • administrātis: = "manage/arrange"; perfect participle → completed passive → "having been + V-en"
  • cum prīmum = "when first/as soon as ..."
  • per: prep. + ACC(X) = "due to X"
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • contendit: = "to hurry/hasten"
16
nūllō hoste prohibente, incolumem legiōnem in Nantuātēs, inde in Allobrogēs perdūxit ibique hiemāvit.
  • incolumem: secondary predication, = "the legion safe" rather than "the safe legion"
  • Nantuātēs: PrN of a Gallic people, Nantuātēs, Nantuātum, m. plural
  • Allobrogēs: PrN of a Gallic people, Allobrogēs, Allobrogum, m. plural
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
17
iīs rēbus cognitīs ā captīvīs perfugīsque, Caesar, praemissō equitātū, cōnfestim legiōnēs subsequī iussit.
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
  • iussit: = "to order"
