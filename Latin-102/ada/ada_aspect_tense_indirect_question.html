Tense and Aspect of Latin Subjunctives in Indirect Statement

Note: Unlike infinitives in indirect statements—where the tense of the infinitive is relative to the matrix verb—the tense of the subjunctive in these constructions carries an absolute temporal and aspectual meaning.

Translational formulae for Latin subjunctives based on time, aspect, and voice.
Subjunctive Tense Aspect / Time Active Voice Passive Voice
Present Simple Present
Formula: base / verb-s [-s(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid Caesar mittat
Eng.: "I know what Caesar sends"
Formula: am/is/are + verb-en [-s(be) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid a Caesare mittatur
Eng.: "I know what is sent by Caesar"
Progressive Present
Formula: is/are + verb-ing [-s(be) + -ing(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid Caesar mittat
Eng.: "I know what Caesar is sending"
Formula: am/is/are + being + verb-en [-s(be) + -ing(be) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid a Caesare mittatur
Eng.: "I know what is being sent by Caesar"
Imperfect Progressive Past
Formula: was/were + verb-ing [-ed(be) + -ing(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid Caesar mitteret
Eng.: "I know what Caesar was sending"
Latin: sciebam quid Caesar mitteret
Eng.: "I knew what Caesar was sending"
Formula: was/were + being + verb-en [-ed(be) + -ing(be) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid a Caesare mitteretur
Eng.: "I know what was being sent by Caesar"
Latin: sciebam quid a Caesare mitteretur
Eng.: "I knew what was being sent by Caesar"
Perfect Simple Past
Formula: verb-ed [-ed(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid Caesar miserit
Eng.: "I know what Caesar sent"
Latin: sciebam quid Caesar miserit
Eng.: "I knew what Caesar sent"
Formula: was/were + verb-en [-ed(be) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid a Caesare missum erit
Eng.: "I know what was sent by Caesar"
Latin: sciebam quid a Caesare missum erit
Eng.: "I knew what was sent by Caesar"
Completed Present
Formula: has/have + verb-en [-s(have) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid Caesar miserit
Eng.: "I know what Caesar has sent"
Formula: has/have + been + verb-en [-s(have) + -en(be) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid a Caesare missum erit
Eng.: "I know what has been sent by Caesar"
Pluperfect Completed Past
Formula: had + verb-en [-ed(have) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid Caesar misisset
Eng.: "I know what Caesar had sent"
Latin: sciebam quid Caesar misisset
Eng.: "I knew what Caesar had sent"
Formula: had + been + verb-en [-ed(have) + -en(be) + -en(verb-x)]
Latin: scio quid a Caesare missum esset
Eng.: "I know what had been sent by Caesar"
Latin: sciebam quid a Caesare missum esset
Eng.: "I knew what had been sent by Caesar"
