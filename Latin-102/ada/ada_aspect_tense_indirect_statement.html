Tense and Aspect of Infinitives in Indirect Statement

Translating the accusative + infinitive construction based on matrix verb time. Future/prospective infinitives are omitted for introductory practice.

A table showing how to translate present and perfect infinitives in indirect statements depending on whether the main verb is present or past tense, grouped by aspect and voice.
Infinitive Form Past Matrix (e.g., dixit) Present Matrix (e.g., dicit)
Present
(mittere / mitti)
Simple Past
Active
Formula: verb-ed
Latin: dixit Caesarem litteras mittere
Eng.: "he said that Caesar sent the letters"
Passive
Formula: was/were + verb-en
Latin: dixit litteras mitti
Eng.: "he said that the letters were sent"
Simple Present
Active
Formula: base / verb-s
Latin: dicit Caesarem litteras mittere
Eng.: "he says that Caesar sends the letters"
Passive
Formula: am/is/are + verb-en
Latin: dicit litteras mitti
Eng.: "he says that the letters are sent"
Progressive Past
Active
Formula: was/were + verb-ing
Latin: dixit Caesarem litteras mittere
Eng.: "he said that Caesar was sending the letters"
If stative, use Simple Past
Passive
Formula: was/were + being + verb-en
Latin: dixit litteras mitti
Eng.: "he said that the letters were being sent"
Progressive Present
Active
Formula: is/are + verb-ing
Latin: dicit Caesarem litteras mittere
Eng.: "he says that Caesar is sending the letters"
If stative, use Simple Present
Passive
Formula: am/is/are + being + verb-en
Latin: dicit litteras mitti
Eng.: "he says that the letters are being sent"
Perfect
(misisse / missas esse)
Completed Past
Active
Formula: had + verb-en
Latin: dixit Caesarem litteras misisse
Eng.: "he said that Caesar had sent the letters"
Passive
Formula: had + been + verb-en
Latin: dixit litteras missas esse
Eng.: "he said that the letters had been sent"
Simple Past
Active
Formula: verb-ed
Latin: dicit Caesarem litteras misisse
Eng.: "he says that Caesar sent the letters"
Passive
Formula: was/were + verb-en
Latin: dicit litteras missas esse
Eng.: "he says that the letters were sent"
Completed Present
Active
Formula: has/have + verb-en
Latin: dicit Caesarem litteras misisse
Eng.: "he says that Caesar has sent the letters"
Passive
Formula: has/have + been + verb-en
Latin: dicit litteras missas esse
Eng.: "he says that the letters have been sent"
